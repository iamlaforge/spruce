# Spruce Context

## Product

The marketing website for Spruce. Spruce is a design reasoning system that installs into AI coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor, and others) to teach them real design thinking and give users creative-director control over the output. The site introduces the product, explains the philosophy behind it, catalogs the commands, and provides installation instructions.

## Audience

Developers and designers who use AI coding tools and have grown frustrated with how generic AI-generated interfaces look. Technically literate, design-aware, and skeptical of marketing fluff — they read READMEs before clicking buttons. Some are hobbyists exploring new tools; others are professionals making decisions about what their team should adopt. The common thread: they care about craft and recognize the problem Spruce addresses.

This audience sees through anything performative. The site itself has to demonstrate the thing it's selling — every choice is an argument for the product.

## Character

Restrained and premium. Quiet confidence, refined execution, the product respects its own space.

For this site specifically, restraint is a design value to be demonstrated, not claimed. No unnecessary ornament, no performance of "designed-ness," no decorative elements that exist only for decoration's sake. Thoughtful spacing and layout hierarchy carry the work. The site should feel like a thoughtful, intuitive system that people want to use — not another landing page.

## Density

Balanced. Moderate density — not spacious enough to read as a consumer lifestyle brand, not dense enough to feel like a utility dashboard. Reading the site should feel unhurried but purposeful.

## Voice

Direct and precise. Minimal words, no hedging, respects the audience's expertise. No marketing throat-clearing, no "revolutionize your workflow," no category clichés. Short sentences that land. The site should explain *why* the product exists without being heavy, and layer tactical examples of how the system works.

## Platform

Web, responsive. Desktop and mobile both first-class.

## Dark mode

Yes — light and dark both first-class. A tasteful dark mode is expected.

## Typography preferences

Open to Spruce's judgment; refine as needed once there's something to work with.

**Explicitly avoid:** the stereotypical AI typefaces — Inter, Geist, and the current crop of geometric grotesques that every AI product defaults to. The site should not read as "made with the same components as every other AI tool."

Given the tradeoff defaults below, typography leans distinctive over classic — character-forward choices are welcome as one of the primary places this restrained interface expresses personality.

## Color preferences

Warm-leaning neutral palette. No specific brand color established yet; accent direction stays open.

**Explicitly avoid:** typical AI accent choices — blue, purple, and especially blue-to-purple gradients. These read as AI-default and undermine the product's positioning.

**Consider:** greens or browns, aligned with the Spruce name. A single confident, non-default accent rather than a loud palette.

## What this should NOT feel like

- Another landing page.
- Heavy, corporate, or overwrought.
- Cluttered, or designed for the sake of appearing designed.
- AI-marketing tropes — blue/purple gradients, hero-with-abstract-blob, "built for the future of X" rhetoric.

Not yet specified. Refine as the site takes shape.

## When Spruce has to decide

**Restraint or expression?** Restraint — quiet, confident, understated.

**Classic or distinctive typography?** Distinctive — character-forward, memorable typographic choices. Given the overall restraint, typography is one of the primary places character shows up.

**Symmetry or tension in layout?** Tension — asymmetric, dynamic layouts over balanced, predictable ones. The other primary place character shows up.

The combination is deliberate: quiet overall, with typography and layout as the expressive moves.
